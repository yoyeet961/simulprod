Create New System:

First, choose which team and sub-team (pod) your system will belong to. The team and pod you choose will affect what kinds of resources you will be able to add to your system- so make sure you know the ownership for the resources you wish to add! For more information about what a 'System' is in DevOps, please check out the full technical specfiication here.

A 'System' can be thought of as a grouping of related resources from an operational standpoint. 'Resources' here can refer to deployable software components, databases, third party services, assemblies, elastic search clusters, and more. Once you fill out all metadata surrounding your new system, you will be able to choose which resources you want to include as a part of it.

Pod Name:

Second, choose a System Name and describe your system. Do your best to fill these out! Adding a good system description can really help another engineer, PM, or curious co-worker understand what it does and how it works. You can also include a google doc or confluence page link if you have written up further information about this system somewhere else.

System Name:

Description: System Page Link (optional):

Third, from an ownership perspective, Systems should have primary and secondary engineering and product points of contact that can be used in the case of an emergency. Emails or slack handle Ids are recommended, since this will make it easier to contact people. If for whatever reason you don't have a primary or secondary engineer, you can simply put 'N/A' in the fields- though this is not recommended. If there are other special processes or ownership details to your system, you can also jot this down in the 'Additional Notes' section

Primary Engineer:

Secondary Engineer:

Product Contact:

Additional Notes (optional):

Finally, it's time to add your resources to your System. This section of the form has been filtered to only show resources owned by the team and pod you selected in the first step of the form. Note that you will not be able to add a resource to your system that already belongs to another. In order to add it to a different system, edit the resource and set it's System to 'None.' Resources can be edited from any of the original /Teams endpoints that list components in DevOps. They can also be edited from the /Teams/Systems View or the /Teams/SystemResources View.
